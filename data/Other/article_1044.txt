GREEN PARTY presidential candidate Jill Stein had no problem raising the $2.5million she wanted to file for ballot recounts in Michigan, Pennsylvania andWisconsin - and now she's looking for more.Stein, who launched the fund-raising effort Wednesday, hit her initial goal andthen upped it a day later to $4.5 million. As of mid-afternoon Thursday, she had$3.94 million.The third party candidate decided to go for a recount after a group of electionlawyers and data experts asked Hillary Clinton's campaign to make sure a hack orcyber attack had not skewed results in the three states, which Clinton lost bysmall margins to Donald Trump. No evidence has emerged that any such attackoccurred. Clinton lost the Electoral College vote to Trump but won the popularvote by more than 2 million.
